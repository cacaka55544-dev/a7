Two-Needle Hand Saddle Stitching vs Machine Sewing

By Atelier Master Artisan • 8 Min Read

High-speed sewing machines use two threads that loop together in the middle of leather (lockstitch). If a single machine thread breaks, the entire seam unravels. **Two-Needle Hand Saddle Stitching** passes two independent waxed linen threads completely through each awl hole in opposite directions. This guide explores hand saddle stitching physics.

1. Structural Redundancy

If a saddle stitch is severed by friction or sharp objects, the opposing thread maintains friction against the leather fibers, keeping the seam secure.

2. Diamond Awl Hole Geometry

Artisans punch diamond-shaped awl holes, slanting stitches at an elegant 45-degree angle that seats linen threads flush against leather surfaces.
